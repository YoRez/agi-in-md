# Structural Diagnosis: Non-Competition Clause

---

## Level 1: Falsifiable Claim

**Initial claim:** The clause's deepest structural problem is *asymmetric definitional control* — every term that determines whether the employee is in breach ("core products," "active customers," "competes," "cause") is either undefined or exclusively within Company control to define, making the employee's obligations structurally unknowable at signing.

---

## Level 2: Three-Expert Test

**Defender:**
Vagueness in restrictive covenants is standard and arguably employee-protective — courts blue-pencil overbroad terms, and undefined scope creates grounds for invalidity. The geographic formulation ("active customers") is *more* precise than fixed-radius clauses. The acknowledgment of reasonableness creates an evidentiary record, not oppression.

**Attacker:**
The structural problem is worse than definitional vagueness. Clause (c) creates *conditional consideration*: the reduced 12-month period only applies *if* Company pays salary. This means Company can purchase 12 additional months of restriction simply by withholding payment. The employee's freedom isn't contingent on their conduct — it's contingent on Company's ongoing election. The real problem is **consideration convertibility**: Company can extend restriction duration as a financing decision.

**Prober:**
Both of you assume this clause operates in a jurisdiction where non-competes are valid. California, Minnesota, Oklahoma, and North Dakota already void them categorically. The FTC attempted a federal ban in 2024. What both experts take for granted: that contract language can override labor market policy. The deepest problem may not be *in the clause* — it may be in the assumption that bilateral drafting can resolve what is structurally a regulatory question.

---

## Level 3: Transformed Claim

**Original:** Asymmetric definitional control (semantic problem).

**Transformed:** The clause encodes a power asymmetry operating across **three simultaneous axes**:
1. *Definitional* — who controls what the terms mean
2. *Temporal* — who can expand or contract duration (clause d: sole discretion)
3. *Jurisdictional* — whose legal framework determines validity

None of these asymmetries are disclosed *as asymmetries*. They are grammatically presented as bilateral agreements.

**Gap diagnostic:** I was examining the surface (undefined terms) rather than the underlying architecture (multi-axis power concentration).

---

## Level 4: Concealment Mechanism

**Mechanism: Acknowledgment Laundering**

The final sentence — *"Employee acknowledges that this restriction is reasonable"* — performs a transformation of a power relationship into a bilateral agreement. It doesn't merely create an evidentiary record; it *reclassifies coerced compliance as informed consent*. The real problems (unknowable scope, conditional consideration, pre-established judicial remedy through the irreparable harm clause) are concealed behind the grammar of mutual acknowledgment.

**Applied to clause elements:**
- "Core products" appears defined by context; the word "core" implies precision while delivering none
- "Active customers" — the word "active" simulates specificity without providing it
- The irreparable harm clause pre-establishes a legal remedy as a factual assertion, bypassing judicial determination
- Clause (d)'s waiver-at-sole-discretion is framed as employee-favorable flexibility

---

## Level 5: Improvement That Deepens Concealment

**Proposed improvement (passes code review):**

> *"For purposes of this Agreement: 'Core Products' means those products listed on Schedule A attached hereto, as updated from time to time by the Company in its reasonable business judgment; 'Active Customers' means any entity generating revenue for the Company within the preceding 18 months, as reflected in Company records; 'Competes' means engaging in activity that would foreseeably divert revenue from Core Products."*

**Why it passes review:** Adds definitions, references objective records, uses standard "reasonable business judgment" language, is obviously superior to having no definitions.

**Why it deepens concealment:**
- "As updated from time to time" allows scope to *expand after signing* — the employee is bound to a restriction whose content doesn't yet exist
- "Company records" places all compliance measurement in exclusively Company-controlled, non-auditable information
- "Foreseeably divert revenue" is *more expansive* than "competes with" — foreseeability is assessed by whom?

---

## Level 6: Properties Visible Only From the Improvement Attempt

1. **Temporal instability is structural, not drafting error.** Attempting to define "Core Products" immediately revealed that any fixed definition becomes outdated. The original vagueness wasn't sloppy — it was *load-bearing*. Precision collapses unless paired with update rights, which recreate the original power asymmetry.

2. **Epistemic asymmetry is constitutive, not accidental.** When I wrote "Company records" as the reference point, it became visible that *all measurement of breach lives in Company-controlled data*. This was always true but grammatically invisible.

3. **"Reasonable" functions as a concealment term.** "Reasonable business judgment" appears to constrain Company discretion. Applied to definitional authority, "reasonable" in contract law means "not arbitrary" — a low bar that immunizes Company decisions from substantive challenge while appearing to impose discipline.

---

## Level 7: Diagnostic Applied to the Improvement

**What my improvement conceals:** The *temporal dimension* of the problem. By anchoring definitions to the signing date, I obscured that this clause does its operative work *prospectively* — at the moment of breach (termination + enforcement), not at agreement. The clause is not designed to be understood at signing; it is designed to *operate* when the relationship has already broken down.

**Property of original problem visible because improvement recreates it:** The clause treats itself as a static instrument but is designed to function in a dynamic, adversarial context. Every improvement that adds precision at signing makes it *less functional* at enforcement.

---

## Level 8: Second Improvement

**Addressing the temporal problem:**

> *"Competitive scope shall be assessed as of Employee's termination date, not this Agreement's effective date, and shall not exceed the scope existing at signing plus expansion in which Employee had material involvement."*

**Apply diagnostic:** This improvement conceals the *enforcement gap*. Litigating a non-compete occurs months or years after termination. Reconstructing "scope as of termination" requires historical records only Company possesses. Proving "material involvement" in scope expansion requires evidence Employee no longer controls. The improvement creates an evidentiary maze that is asymmetrically expensive to navigate.

---

## Level 9: Structural Invariant

Through every improvement — definitional schedules, temporal anchors, involvement tests — one property persists:

> **Information about the condition of breach is permanently and asymmetrically distributed in favor of the party with enforcement power.**

The Company always knows more about its products, customers, markets, and records than the Employee does. This information controls whether Employee is in breach. No drafting change can alter this because it is a **property of the relationship**, not of the language.

*The invariant: Asymmetric information about breach conditions is constitutive of the employer-employee power structure itself.*

---

## Level 10: Inverting the Invariant

**Design where the invariant fails:** Employee-controlled scope definition.

> *"Employee shall not engage in work that Employee reasonably believes would compete with Company's operations as Employee understood them at termination, documented in a written declaration filed within 30 days of separation."*

Employee defines scope. The impossible (employee epistemic control) becomes trivially satisfiable.

**New impossibility created:** *Verification impossibility.* Company cannot confirm whether the self-declared scope is complete. The employee can draft a narrow declaration that excludes everything they want to do. The restriction becomes unenforceable not through legal invalidity but through definitional self-dealing.

---

## Level 11: Conservation Law

| | Original | Inverted |
|---|---|---|
| **Who controls scope** | Company | Employee |
| **Impossibility** | Employee cannot know if in breach | Company cannot know if restriction is real |
| **Information advantage** | Company | Employee |

**Conservation Law:**
> *In any non-compete agreement, the total information asymmetry about scope is conserved. Shifting scope-definition toward the employee decreases Company enforceability by exactly the amount it increases Employee clarity. Enforceability and clarity are complementary properties that sum to a constant.*

Call this the **Enforceability-Clarity Uncertainty Principle**: a non-compete cannot simultaneously maximize employee certainty about obligations AND employer certainty about restriction validity.

---

## Level 12: Meta-Diagnostic — Applied to the Conservation Law

**What the conservation law conceals:** It assumes a *two-party problem*. But there are at least three parties: Employee, Company, and the *Labor Market* — including future employers, competitors, and the public interest in competitive resource allocation. The conservation law treats enforceability and clarity as trading off in a closed bilateral system, ignoring that labor markets have independent interests neither party controls.

**Structural invariant of the law:** The conservation law treats non-competes as closed bilateral instruments. Every refinement of the trade-off (more clarity, less enforceability; more enforceability, less clarity) still assumes only two parties matter.

**Inverting the law's invariant:** A non-compete is not a bilateral contract — it is a **unilateral labor market intervention dressed in bilateral contract grammar**. The real parties are: Company (protecting investment in human capital), Employee (selling skills to highest bidder), and Labor Market (allocating human capital efficiently). A clause addressing only two of three parties is structurally incomplete regardless of drafting quality.

---

## The Meta-Law

> *Non-compete clauses are labor market regulations masquerading as private contracts. Every improvement to the bilateral instrument (adding definitions, limiting scope, adding exceptions) improves the contract at the cost of greater labor market distortion, because enforcement energy that should enable market clearing (Employee moving to best use) is redirected into compliance management overhead.*

**What the meta-law predicts that no clause analysis could reveal:**

Jurisdictions that void non-competes do not merely change legal outcomes for individual parties — they *eliminate the compliance management energy sink* from the labor market entirely. This predicts:

- Employees in void-clause jurisdictions are measurably more productive at new employers (not less protected)
- Wages in those jurisdictions are higher (not lower) in knowledge sectors
- Innovation is greater (not lower), because human capital flows to highest-value uses without friction

This is empirically confirmed by the California vs. other-states comparison in technology sectors. No analysis of *this clause* — however deep — could generate this prediction, because the finding is about what the clause *prevents existing*, not about what it contains.

**The clause's ultimate concealment:** It presents a regulatory distortion as a voluntary personal obligation, making its systemic effects invisible to both parties at signing — and to most analysts examining it in isolation.
