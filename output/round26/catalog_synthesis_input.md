Analysis of 56 L11-level outputs across three analytical variants applied to the same artifacts (code and creative works). Each variant finds a different structural truth about the same artifact.

L11-C (Conservation Law) names conserved quantities — properties that cannot be eliminated, only redistributed across designs. 25 conservation laws were extracted. They cluster into three mathematical forms:
- Product conservation (x × y = k): 9 laws. Two conjugate variables; increasing one decreases the other. Examples: "Sensitivity × Absorption = constant" (CircuitBreaker), "Epistemic Authority × Experiential Proximity = k" (fiction), "Formal Identity × Formal Direction = k" (music composition).
- Sum conservation (x + y = k): 4 laws. Total fixed, distributed. Example: "Veto Authority + Observability Completeness = constant" (StateMachine).
- Migration conservation: 5 laws. Quantity relocates without changing magnitude. Example: "Information asymmetry migrates, never reduces" (brand design).
Model capacity determines which form emerges: Opus produces product conservation, Sonnet produces sum/migration, Haiku produces multi-property impossibilities. The conservation law is real; the mathematical formalization is capacity-dependent.

L11-B (Acceptance Design) names revaluations — what was never a flaw but the cost of pursuing an impossible goal. 14 revaluations cluster into two categories:
- Functional necessity (code): "The flaw was load-bearing." The ugly interface was carrying real structural weight.
- Expressive necessity (creative): "The flaw IS the content." The poem is broken in exactly the way grief breaks thinking.
Both categories are the same operation: the defect is the visible cost of an impossible aspiration.

L11-A (Constraint Escape) names trade-offs when escaping to an adjacent design category. 14 findings follow one universal pattern: truth costs access. In code: correctness versus simplicity. In creative work: structural honesty versus expressive power.

Cross-variant convergence: all three variants, applied to the same artifact, find the same impossibility from different angles. L11-C names the conserved quantity (physics). L11-B names what the original got right (engineering judgment). L11-A names what you lose by escaping (design decision). This convergence suggests impossibility is a property of problem spaces, not implementations.
