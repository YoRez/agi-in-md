# Opus Vanilla

Input: Across all todo apps — different UIs, features, target users, decades of design iteration — what property of work management never changes? What is the conserved quantity that every todo app preserves, even the ones that try to break every convention?

---

**Attention debt.**

Every todo app — regardless of UI, philosophy, or era — conserves the total obligation created by capturing an item. When something enters the system as "open," it creates a unit of attention debt. That debt never vanishes. It can only be:

- **Discharged** (completed)
- **Transferred** (delegated)
- **Deferred** (snoozed, rescheduled, deprioritized)
- **Written off** (deleted, archived, abandoned)

But it cannot be destroyed. The sum is always zero: every item that enters must exit through one of those four channels. Apps that hide items to "reduce overwhelm" haven't reduced the debt — they've deferred it. Apps that auto-archive haven't eliminated it — they've written it off. The accounting always balances.

This is why every todo app, no matter how minimal, must implement at minimum two states (open/closed) and why every "revolutionary" todo app that tries to eliminate the checkbox just moves the discharge mechanism somewhere else — a swipe, a drag, an expiration, a calendar boundary. The gesture changes. The conservation law doesn't.

The deeper implication: **the thing being managed was never the task. It was the attention claim the task makes on you.** The apps that feel best are the ones whose write-off channel has the lowest friction — they make it easy to honestly acknowledge "this will never happen" without forcing you to call it "done."
